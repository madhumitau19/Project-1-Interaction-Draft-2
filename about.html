This is an exercise in embracing randomness and creating something interesting with limited control, allowing chance to take over. It works similarly to Dada cut-out poetry or blackout poetry, where you use existing writing to create something new spontaneously. You can see the product as a unique piece of art, or a poem of your own, or whatever you please.

The specific poems used are: The Passionate Shepherd to His Love by Christopher Marlowe and Sir Walter Raleigh’s response to it, The Nymph’s Reply to the Shepherd. The words from these poems will be used to create a random unique design. You can then print this design and draw connections between words to craft your own poem, as a third character of sorts in this interaction between Marlowe and Raleigh.
